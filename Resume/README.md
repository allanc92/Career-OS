# Your master resume

Your master resume is the canonical source for employment, education, dates, skills, and other experience facts. Every job-specific resume starts here.

## Add it during onboarding

Place your most complete current resume in this folder. Common choices include:

- `Master_Resume.md`
- `Master_Resume.docx`
- `Master_Resume.pdf`

The real file is personal and ignored by Git. `Master_Resume.example.md` is a blank public template and remains tracked.

If possible, use Markdown or another text-readable format so your assistant can reliably read and update it. You do not need to know Markdown; ask your assistant to create it from the resume you provide, then review the result carefully.

## What belongs in the master

Keep the fullest verified version of:

- contact and location details
- role titles, organizations, and dates
- scope and responsibilities
- accomplishments with evidence
- education and credentials
- skills, tools, languages, publications, or portfolios when relevant

Do not add a claim merely because it sounds useful. Mark missing dates, numbers, or details as unknown and resolve them later.

## Tailored resumes are outputs

For an application, the assistant should:

1. Read the master resume.
2. Read the job description.
3. Select and reorder relevant verified evidence.
4. Use the StoryBank and Achievement Log for supported detail.
5. Ask about any missing claim.
6. Show the tailored draft for approval.
7. Save it in that private application's folder.

A tailored resume must never replace the master resume. Add a new fact to the master only after you confirm it.

## Keep it current

Ask:

```text
Review my recent approved achievements against my master resume. Suggest facts
that should be added, but do not edit the master until I approve each change.
```
