# Why Career OS works

Career OS is not a chatbot history. It is a set of plain files that you own and can inspect.

## Context should persist

A chat starts with whatever you remember to paste. Career OS gives a file-capable assistant durable context: your goals, experience, wins, stories, applications, and decisions. Each approved update remains available next time.

The important requirement is an AI that can read AND write the folder. Reading without writing leaves the system stale. Writing without reading loses the context that makes the work accurate.

## Facts come before fluency

AI can make unsupported statements sound convincing. Career OS reverses that priority:

1. Find the relevant source file.
2. Use only supported facts.
3. Ask when evidence is missing.
4. Show important drafts before saving.

Your master resume anchors work history. Your profile anchors direction and voice. Your Achievement Log anchors outcomes. Your StoryBank anchors accomplishment stories. Your Pipeline anchors current status.

## One source for each kind of truth

When the same fact appears in many places, it drifts. Career OS names a canonical source for each kind of information and treats tailored documents as outputs.

For example, `Resume/Master_Resume.*` is the starting point for every job-specific resume. A tailored resume may emphasize different evidence, but it must not silently rewrite the master.

## The system compounds

Career OS becomes more useful through a simple loop:

1. Work or a job search produces a new fact.
2. You ask the assistant to capture it.
3. The assistant asks for evidence and missing context.
4. You approve the update.
5. Future work can reuse the stronger source.

A win can enter the Achievement Log, become a StoryBank outline, develop into a verified STAR story, support a resume bullet, and later help prepare for an interview.

## The StoryBank is a living asset

The StoryBank lifecycle is visible:

- Onboarding seeds outlines from accomplishments you describe.
- The StoryBank builder develops an outline through questions.
- `StoryBank_Pipeline.md` tracks developing and ready stories.
- Application work selects accurate evidence for a role.
- Interview preparation selects stories for likely questions.
- Your own written notes after a conversation can suggest an approved improvement.

Stories are never reconstructed from vague model memory. They grow from your facts.

## Thorough onboarding is a feature

Career direction depends on more than job titles. The onboarding interview explores your career arc, interests, energy, strengths, constraints, goals, voice, wins, and evidence. That depth gives later recommendations a foundation.

It is intentionally not a speed-optimized setup. It is also not final. You can add context or change direction at any time.

## Human approval remains central

The assistant can organize, compare, and draft. You decide what is true, what sounds like you, what should be saved, and what should be shared. Career OS supports judgment rather than replacing it.

## Privacy is part of the design

Personal files are ignored by Git by default, and the template contains no real person's career data. That protects against an accidental GitHub upload when the rules are followed.

It does not stop an AI vendor from processing files you let its tool read. Choose a tool and account whose privacy terms fit your information. See [Privacy and your data](privacy.md).
