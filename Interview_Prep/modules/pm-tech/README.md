# PM and Tech Interview Practice

This is an optional specialization module for product management and technology
roles. Nothing else in Career OS depends on it. If it does not match your target
roles, delete this entire folder.

## What is here

- `Product_Sense_Framework.md`: a practical structure for ambiguous product
  design and improvement questions.
- `Product_Execution_Framework.md`: a practical structure for diagnosis,
  prioritization, delivery, and learning questions.
- `Practice_Log.example.md`: a copyable reflection log for deliberate practice.

The frameworks are level-agnostic. Use the depth, scope, and vocabulary that fit
the role and prompt rather than trying to signal a particular title.

## How to practice

1. Pick one sample prompt or write your own.
2. Work through it without reading the framework.
3. Immediately write notes in your own words about what you said, decided,
   skipped, and found difficult.
4. Compare those notes with the relevant framework and checklist.
5. Copy `Practice_Log.example.md` to `Practice_Log.md` and add one entry.
6. Choose one specific improvement to practice next.

Keep every reflection grounded in notes you personally wrote. If a detail is not
in those notes, mark it unknown rather than reconstructing or inventing it. An
assistant may organize or critique your notes, but it should not add claims
about what happened.

## Sample coaching prompts

```text
Using only the practice notes below, identify one strong decision, one unclear
decision, and one focused drill for next time. Do not infer anything that is not
in my notes.
```

```text
Compare my notes with the checklist in Product_Sense_Framework.md. Separate
direct evidence from your interpretation, and ask questions where the notes are
incomplete.
```

```text
Review my notes with Product_Execution_Framework.md. Show where I connected a
decision to evidence, where I made an unsupported assumption, and what I should
practice next. Use no facts beyond my notes.
```

## Create another specialization module

A module should be useful on its own and safe to remove.

1. Create a new folder under `Interview_Prep/modules/` with a short role or
   domain name.
2. Add a `README.md` that states who it is for, what it contains, and that it is
   optional and deletable.
3. Add one or two concise frameworks for recurring interview tasks in that
   field.
4. Include realistic, generic sample prompts with no private or employer
   information.
5. Add a copyable practice log that relies only on the user's own written
   notes.
6. Keep links and dependencies inside the module. Do not require changes to the
   Career OS core.
7. Delete the folder as a test. The rest of Career OS should still work.

Modules are content packs, not agent skills. Keep repeatable agent behavior in
the system's shared skill area rather than creating skills here.
